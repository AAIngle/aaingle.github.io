Stray
Image sourced from Stray Steam page

Synopsis

A stray cat accidentally finds itself in an abandonded futuristic walled city -- no longer inhabited by humans, but by bots. In order to return to the outside, this cat must explore the city, communicate with bots for help, and observe the remnants of humanity.

Review

Maybe I'm in the dark about cat-type games, but I'm really surprised there aren't more of them. It's unsurprisingly charming to play as this willful kitty in this walled-in cyberpunk city. Even though this protagonist is a bit more mysterious than usual, since this cat can't talk, the music is a more expressive companion. I interpreted the music as a window into the cat's mindsent -- it's curious, percussive, ebbing, flowing, and responsive. Apart from the protagonist though, the environment design is the real main event. Every piece of clutter makes the place feel alive, and every place's clutter has a unique kind of life on display. One location is filled with litter, old woven cloth, and rusted metal; another with crates of goods, shiny plastic clothes, and neon lights; and another with flowers, wood, and string lights. I wanted to soak in every inch of each major location. My only complaint is that movement as the cat was more limited than I expected. For example, you can't jump onto just anything you want -- a button prompt is what allows the jump to happen. I'm understanding of limitations in games, especially in the context of indie games, but the disappointment was still present. I got over it fairly quickly, but that doesn't stop me from imagining a freer experience while exploring.

The story has its strengths, although I understand why most people didn't pay it much mind because the atmosphere and world are just that good. Great atmosphere and world design is both obvious and seemingly effortless -- people may not be able to point out the details that make it good, but they know a strong atmosphere instinctually. The plot you guide the cat through ends up feeling like only a small part of a big world. It won't appeal to everyone's taste, but I think that's perfect for a story about a cat.
